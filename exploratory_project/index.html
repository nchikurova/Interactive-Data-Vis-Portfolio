With a final number of 28,616 infected people, including 11,310 deaths (case-fatality rate of 40%), The Western African Ebola virus epidemic was the most widespread outbreak of Ebola virus disease (EVD) in history—causing major loss of life and socioeconomic disruption in the region, mainly in Guinea, Liberia, and Sierra Leone in 2013 – 2016.
